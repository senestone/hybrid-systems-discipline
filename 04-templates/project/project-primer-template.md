---

# 1. Problem Statement

Clearly define:

- The problem being addressed  
- Why it matters  
- Who is impacted  
- The cost of inaction  

This section SHALL describe the problem only.

Solution language is prohibited at this stage.

---

# 2. Desired Outcomes

Define:

- Observable success conditions  
- Measurable impact (if known)  
- Conceptual acceptance criteria  

Outcomes SHALL describe results, not system structure.

---

# 3. Stakeholders

Identify:

- Primary stakeholders  
- Secondary stakeholders  
- Decision makers  
- Operational owners  
- Compliance stakeholders (if applicable)  

Stakeholder misalignment is a lifecycle risk.

---

# 4. Context and Environment

Describe:

- Business context  
- Existing systems  
- Integration boundaries  
- Organizational constraints  
- Regulatory considerations (if applicable)  
- Operational environment  

Context informs feasibility, not design.

---

# 5. Constraints

Document:

- Technical constraints  
- Resource constraints  
- Timeline constraints  
- Infrastructure limitations  
- Governance requirements  
- Compliance requirements  

Constraints SHALL be recorded prior to architectural discussion.

---

# 6. Risks and Unknowns

Identify:

- Technical risks  
- Organizational risks  
- Dependency risks  
- Regulatory risks  
- Probabilistic integration risks (if AI components are anticipated)  
- Areas of uncertainty  

Classify risk level:

- Low  
- Moderate  
- High  

Unclassified risks indicate incomplete ideation.

---

# 7. Preliminary Non-Functional Considerations

At a conceptual level, identify:

- Performance sensitivity  
- Scalability expectations  
- Security implications  
- Reliability posture  
- Auditability expectations  
- Reproducibility requirements  
- Maintainability concerns  

These SHALL inform the Requirements phase.

They SHALL NOT trigger architectural definition.

---

# 8. Deterministic–Probabilistic Awareness (If Applicable)

If AI or probabilistic components are anticipated, document:

- Nature of probabilistic behavior expected  
- Areas of uncertainty  
- Potential containment challenges  
- Validation concerns  
- Governance risks  

This section does not authorize architectural modeling.

It identifies structural risk.

---

# 9. Alternative Solution Directions

Describe at least two conceptual directions (if applicable):

- Conceptual Option A  
- Conceptual Option B  
- Trade-offs  
- Risk differences  
- Open questions  

Selection of a preferred option is prohibited in Ideation.

Premature convergence introduces lifecycle risk.

---

# 10. Preliminary Scope Boundaries

Clarify:

- Likely in-scope areas  
- Clearly out-of-scope areas  
- Areas requiring further validation  
- Explicit exclusions  

Scope clarity reduces drift.

---

# 11. Lifecycle Readiness Assessment

Before advancing to Requirements phase, confirm:

- Problem is clearly articulated  
- Outcomes are defined  
- Constraints are documented  
- Risks are surfaced and classified  
- Alternative approaches explored  
- No architectural commitments made  
- No implementation assumptions embedded  
- RTM scaffold prepared  

If any condition is unmet, remain in Ideation.

Advancement requires explicit human authorization.

# Governance Notes

The Project Primer:

- Defines intent  
- Surfaces structural risk  
- Prevents premature architecture  
- Prevents solution-first thinking  
- Anchors lifecycle discipline  

It SHALL NOT:

- Define system architecture  
- Select technologies  
- Define interfaces  
- Specify data models  
- Authorize implementation  

Violation of Ideation discipline increases downstream rework.
